# Voxtyper

### Your voice is the keyboard.

Voxtyper is a hands-free, polyglot dictation companion for macOS that turns your voice — in any of thirteen languages — into pristine text in any app, and lets you trigger scripts just by saying the word.

---

## Speak. Watch the words appear.

Most dictation tools force you into their own text box. Voxtyper doesn't. It's a tiny menu-bar app that waits for your hotkey, listens, and drops the transcribed text straight into whatever app you're already using — Slack, Mail, Safari, your IDE, ChatGPT, a terminal.

No window switching. No copy-paste. No "send" button. You speak, and it appears.

---

## What makes Voxtyper different

### The cursor is the canvas
Whatever app you're already in *is* the dictation target. Voxtyper pastes natively into the focused field using macOS accessibility APIs.

### Your tongue is not a bug
Speak Persian, Japanese, German, Arabic, Spanish — Voxtyper handles thirteen languages out of the box, and can translate to English on the fly so it doesn't matter what language you happen to be thinking in.

### Words can do things
Register a keyword and a shell command. Say "lock screen" and your Mac sleeps the display. Say "open Slack" and Slack launches. Say "deploy staging" and your deploy script runs. Your voice becomes a launcher.

### Real-time, not after-the-fact
Words appear as soon as you've spoken them, thanks to streaming transcription. There's no waiting for the silence-then-process pause that other dictation tools impose.

### Auto-submit with one word
End any phrase with **search** and Voxtyper presses Return after pasting. Perfect for Google, ChatGPT, Spotlight, or any search box.

## Privacy, in plain language

- Audio goes **directly** from your Mac to OpenAI. There's no Voxtyper server.
- No analytics. No telemetry. No tracking.
- Your API key stays on your machine.
- The microphone is only active while the menu-bar icon is red.
